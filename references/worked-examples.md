# Worked Examples

Use these when the right focus is not obvious.

## 1. Build More Features vs Fix Activation

Situation:
`The team wants to add 5 new features, but most users never complete onboarding.`

- Better focus: fix onboarding activation first
- Why: new features do not matter if users never reach value
- Action: `do now`
- What to ignore: broad feature expansion

## 2. Infrastructure Upgrade vs Shipping The Core Flow

Situation:
`The team wants to move to a more complex architecture before proving demand.`

- Better focus: protect the shortest path to learning
- Why: premature infrastructure adds carry-cost before the bottleneck is real
- Action: `defer` or `simplify`

## 3. More Research vs Decision-Relevant Research

Situation:
`There are many interesting questions, but only one unknown changes the launch decision.`

- Better focus: research the decision-changing unknown
- Why: broad curiosity is lower leverage than clarity on the gating risk
- Action: `measure first`

## 4. Many Small Polish Tasks vs One Blocking UX Fix

Situation:
`A lot of minor polish work is queued, but one confusing checkout step causes most drop-off.`

- Better focus: fix the blocking step
- Why: one flow dominates the outcome
- Action: `do now`
- What to ignore: broad polish spread evenly across the product

## 5. High Leverage Does Not Mean Build Immediately

Situation:
`A new enterprise feature could matter a lot, but confidence is low and sales evidence is weak.`

- Better focus: test first
- Why: the upside may be large, but direct build cost is too high relative to certainty
- Action: `test first`
